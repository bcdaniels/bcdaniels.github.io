What does it mean to understand?

Arthur Lander gave a great after-dinner talk at the 2012 q-bio Conference that jived with my recent thoughts --- the goal of science is not to enumerate facts, but to find heuristics that provide cognitive shortcuts, to sharpen our intuition so that we don't have to remember so many facts. Or, to quote David Krakauer, the goal is "not to explain a lot with a lot, but to explain a lot with a little."

[everyday example] In this sense, your understanding of a phenomenon can be described as a probability distribution describing your belief in the likelihood that different events will occur.

Probability distributions are the playthings of statistical physicists. [connect to physical models? "physics is just commmon sense."]
